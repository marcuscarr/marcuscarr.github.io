Data Science

My favorite parts of grad school were when I could finally sit down, dig through the data I had collected, and try to make sense of it all. Afterwards, I got curious about what can be done with much, much larger data sets and started improving my data science skill set. Most of this work has been through Coursera (my profile), as well as UC Berkeley's Intro CS: CS61A series.

I used Python in graduate school, and Python is also the main language in CS61A. The Coursera work has been in R. At my day job, we use a little bit of SQL.

Coursera Data Science Specialization

The Data Science Specialization is a collection of nine 4-week courses and a capstone project. Most of the courses have associated mini-projects. I've completed eight of the nine, and the mini-projects are posted at my GitHub.

One project explores the built-in trees data set in R. You can read about the app and try it for yourself.

Projects

Two projects in progress, one focused on visualizing geographic data and the other on exercise habits gleaned from social media. Coming soon!

Biochemistry Research

I was a PhD student with Judith Klinman in the Department of Chemistry at UC Berkeley. Broadly speaking, I studied biochemistry and structural biology. More specifically, the structure and kinetics of a couple lipoxygenases. Some of the structures have been deposited to the PDB: 4WFO, 4WHA.
